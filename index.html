Our Story

From nervous newcomers to confident graduates - our journey through the halls of PDS Jand

The Batch That Became Family

In 2017, 90 young students entered the gates of Punjab Daanish School Jand Attock, not knowing they were about to embark on a journey that would shape their lives forever. As a boarding school, PDS Jand wasn't just a place of education - it became our home.

Through shared dormitories, late-night study sessions, weekend activities, and countless inside jokes, we transformed from classmates to brothers. The bonds formed in these halls are the kind that last a lifetime.

From the academic blocks to the sports fields, from the dining hall to the dormitories - every corner of this campus holds memories of laughter, learning, and the occasional mischief that made our time here truly special.

Our Timeline

The journey of Batch 6 from 2017 to 2024

2017

The Beginning

90 nervous but excited students joined PDS Jand as Class 6. The first days were filled with homesickness and new friendships forming in equal measure. We began learning the rhythms of boarding school life together.

2018

Finding Our Footing

By Class 7, we'd settled into boarding school life. Dormitory routines were established, and batch identity began to take shape. We started discovering each other's talents and personalities.

2019

Coming Together

As Class 8 students, we started taking leadership roles in school activities. Our batch won several inter-house competitions. The bonds of friendship grew stronger through shared experiences.

2020

The Challenge

COVID-19 disrupted our Class 9 year, but we adapted to online learning while supporting each other through the difficult times. Even apart, our batch remained connected.

2021-2022

Senior Years

As Class 10 and 11 students, we took on mentorship roles for younger batches while preparing for our board examinations. Our batch became known for both academic excellence and strong camaraderie.

2023-2024

Graduation

Our final years as Class 12 students were bittersweet - proud of our accomplishments but sad to leave our second home. Batch 6 graduated, but the bonds remain unbroken.
